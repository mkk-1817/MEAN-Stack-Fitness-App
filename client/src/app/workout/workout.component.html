Exercise Workouts
1. Strength Training: Engage in weight lifting, bodyweight exercises (like push-ups, pull-ups), and resistance band workouts 3-4 times a week to build muscle mass.
2. Moderate Cardio: Perform light to moderate cardio like brisk walking, swimming, or cycling for 20-30 minutes, 2-3 times a week to improve cardiovascular health without excessive calorie burning.
3. Flexibility and Balance: Incorporate yoga or Pilates 1-2 times a week to improve flexibility and balance.

1. Cardio: Engage in moderate to vigorous cardio exercises like brisk walking, jogging, swimming, or cycling for at least 150 minutes per week, gradually increasing to 300 minutes.
2. Strength Training: Incorporate strength training exercises 2-3 times a week to build muscle and boost metabolism.
3. Low-Impact Activities: For those with joint issues, focus on low-impact activities like swimming, elliptical training, or water aerobics.
4. Flexibility and Mobility: Perform stretching and mobility exercises to improve flexibility and reduce injury risk.
5. Functional Movements: Include functional training that mimics daily activities to improve overall movement and strength.
General Tips for All BMIs
  • - Consult Professionals: Before starting any diet or exercise program, consult with healthcare providers or dietitians to tailor plans to individual needs and conditions.
  • - Stay Consistent: Consistency is key in both diet and exercise to achieve and maintain health goals.
  • - Listen to Your Body: Adjust your plan based on how your body responds, ensuring you don’t overexert or under-nourish yourself.
